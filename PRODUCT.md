# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Operations and supply chain professionals using Inact — inventory managers, procurement leads, supplier coordinators. Enterprise context: they use Inact daily and turn to the learning platform to get better at it. They are capable adults, not students. They learn between tasks, often in short sessions, and expect the tool to respect their time and intelligence.

## Product Purpose

A learning platform built into the Inact ecosystem. Delivers modular, self-paced courses on inventory management, supplier management, and Inact fundamentals. Success looks like users completing a learning path, earning a certificate, and feeling meaningfully more capable in Inact afterward.

## Brand Personality

Professional, clean, trustworthy, approachable. The platform should feel like a well-designed internal tool that takes the user seriously — not a consumer app, not a corporate compliance portal.

## Anti-references

- **LinkedIn Learning**: clunky, corporate, overly structured, heavy chrome. The platform should feel lighter and more considered.
- **Duolingo / Kahoot-style gamification**: childish, cartoonish, reward loops that feel manipulative. Achievements and progress should feel earned and meaningful, not hollow.
- **Generic SaaS dashboards**: metric-heavy, data-for-data's-sake layouts. Learning context is human, not analytical.

## Design Principles

1. **Progress should feel earned.** Gamification elements (badges, certificates, completion states) carry weight because they're understated. Restraint makes them meaningful.
2. **Clean signal, not empty space.** Whitespace and simplicity serve clarity, not decoration. Every element earns its place by reducing friction or communicating something real.
3. **Product, not platform.** The experience should feel like an extension of Inact — same trust, same professionalism — not a bolted-on third-party LMS.
4. **Warmth without informality.** Approachable tone and visual softness, but never casual or playful to the point of undermining credibility.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Standard keyboard navigation and focus management. No motion-only affordances. Color contrast checked at all text/background pairings.
