[id:2/topic: The ABC Analysis]

slide_id: af794ea8-63bd-46e7-83c1-00c499a4b7dc
index_title: The Vital Few and the Trivial Many
title: ABC Analysis: The Vital Few and the Trivial Many
type: main
layout: standard-2-col
---
**To understand double ABC analysis, it helps to first recall the basics of classic ABC analysis.**

ABC analysis is based on the Pareto principle (the 80/20 rule): a small share of items usually accounts for most of the total value.

We see this pattern in everyday life. Most people wear about 20% of their clothes 80% of the time, and we typically call 20% of our contacts most frequently.

In inventory management, the same logic applies. In a warehouse, a small number of products (A items) often generate the majority of sales or inventory value, while a larger number of products (B and C items) contribute significantly less.

![image/size:500px/align:right/frame:none](learn/images/26/5cd248fcaf79ef7027a40c1579d64625ffa2fa13d4452a7b4f3e4d3f9d918b61)
---

slide_id: 8e92a5a5-96f8-441f-a100-632982d67fbe
index_title: ABC Analysis
title: ABC Analysis
type: main
layout: standard-2-col
---
If we for example look at our products demand, we see that a small share of products accounts for most of the demand, while most products contribute very little.

**We can group them into the three categories:**

* **A products** – the top ~10% of products that make up 80% of total demand
* **B products** – the next ~20% of products that cover 15% of demand
* **C products** – the remaining ~70% of products that add up to only 5% of demand

And that’s the idea behind ABC analysis: a few products matter most, while most matter much less.

![image/size:500px/align:right/frame:none](learn/images/25/04958bd49fb433f8dcf28688f56011e7d1e0670bc4bdaa9bdf8de1fe033cc7c2)

[id:3/topic: The Double ABC Analysis]

slide_id: e72c0b55-2990-4bf9-84b2-06237b7b21ad
index_title: Introducing the Double ABC Analysis
title: Introducing the Double ABC Analysis
type: main
layout: standard-2-col
---
Now you know the theory behind an ABC analysis, but there’s more:

**The double ABC analysis looks at two variables at the same time instead of just one.** In the inventory solution in Inact Now, these are:

**1. Cost of inventory** – how much the product cost us over the last 12 months (total cost of goods).
**2. Demand** – how often the product was sold or used in the last 12 months (consumption lines).

![image/size:500px/align:right/frame:none](learn/images/28/8aa6d8fa6bccecb9796f7cef2a3ae8077df0d28f6bf110c3822c3988fba066e6)
---
> [next] Continue -> 24b27fec-bf4f-426e-a988-e9090e6a3023

slide_id: 24b27fec-bf4f-426e-a988-e9090e6a3023
index_title: Understanding the Double ABC Analysis
title: Understanding the Double ABC Analysis
type: main
layout: standard-2-col
---
This is how it works: 

Each product gets **two scores**, A / B / C, for each variable. These two scores are combined into a two-letter category. For example:

* **AA** = high cost of inventory, high demand
* **CB** = low cost of inventory, medium demand

This helps you quickly see which products are the most important and decide how to manage them.

![image/size:500px/frame:none/align:right](learn/images/16/eed5f4b5f8c3b8dd51502fc1202767aae8223a363bafd7453952dfe49b26fd3a)

**And just like that, every product in your inventory will be assigned a double ABC category.**

Where a single ABC analysis may look at demand alone, double ABC adds a second lens: cost of inventory. It shows you both how predictable a products demand is - and how expensive it is to hold. 

**Note:** Cost of inventory and demand are just examples of variables you can use for the analysis. The best choice of variables will always depend on your specific goals.

[id:5/topic: Special Categories]

slide_id: 7353f3a6-6861-48b6-b453-9fde8581322e
index_title: What Are Special Categories?
title: What About Items That Don’t Quite Fit?
type: main
layout: standard-2-col
---
When people first see their double ABC analysis, a common question often comes up: **“What about items that don’t quite fit?”**

For example, new products often appear as low-performing (like CC) simply because they haven’t had time to generate sales. That doesn’t mean they lack potential.

That’s why the Inact Now view goes **beyond the standard nine categories.** We’ve added **five extra classifications** to capture special cases—like new, discontinued, or strategic items—so you can act with more nuance.
