[id:2/topic: The Trap]

slide_id: 2
index_title: The Trap of Routine Purchasing
title: **The Trap of Routine Purchasing**
type: main
---
Most of the time, purchasing feels like a routine, reactive task.

When things get busy, it is easy to focus only on the immediate needs: placing orders to fill empty shelves and putting out daily fires just to keep the business running.

This is the trap of routine purchasing. It keeps the lights on, but it keeps procurement acting as a simple support function rather than looking at the bigger picture.

[id:3/topic: The Good News]

slide_id: 4
index_title: The Good News
title: **The Good News**
type: main
---
The good news is that these symptoms aren’t unfixable - organizations can evolve through different levels of maturity over time.

As an organization grows, procurement needs to grow with it. The focus shifts from chasing the lowest price to coordinating sourcing efforts, lowering total cost of ownership, and eventually optimizing the entire value chain.

We must actively move along this roadmap from being a reactive order-handler to a strategic partner. But how do we do that?
