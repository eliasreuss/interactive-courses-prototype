index_title: What are Insights?
title: **What are Insights?**
type: main
---
Insights allow you to deeply analyze specific areas of your supply chain. You can think of them as highly interactive reports.

**You will encounter two main types in the platform:**

-**Pre-built templates:** Ready-to-use analyses covering the most common procurement and inventory use cases.
-**Custom Insights:** Tailored analyses built specifically by your company to match your unique workflows.

Most importantly, every Insight connects directly to your organization’s ERP system and updates automatically every day. This ensures that regardless of the Insight you are using, you are always turning your most accurate, up-to-date data into clear answers.

[id:3/topic: Slicers and filters]

slide_id: 5
index_title: What are slicers and filters?
title: **What are slicers and filters?**
type: main
---
Now that you know how to change **how** your data is displayed using views, let’s look at how to control **what** data is displayed.

Looking at your entire supply chain at once can be overwhelming. Slicers and filters allow you to cut through the noise by segmenting your data based on broader parameters. You can slice and filter on almost anything, meaning the specific options available will change depending on which insight you are using.

For example, you can choose to look only at your "A" products or filter for all items that have a reorder point below a certain amount. Ultimately, these tools help you narrow down your focus to the segments that actually matter.

[id:4/topic: One click deeper]

slide_id: 7
index_title: One click deeper
title: **One click deeper**
type: main
---
Filtering your data is a great start, but true transparency requires going from broad categories down to the atomic level. We call this going one click deeper.

Let's say you are looking at your double ABC analysis. You want to see your most critical items, so you click on the AA category. You then sort the items by total cost of goods consumed to find the biggest contributor. From there, you click directly on the top item to dive into its individual performance. This process takes you from a broad category right down to the atomic level.
