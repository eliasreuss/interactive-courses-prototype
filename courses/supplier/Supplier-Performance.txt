[id:2/topic: The problem]

slide_id: 2
index_title: Not all suppliers are created equal.
title: **Not all suppliers are created equal**
type: main
---
As suppliers become a critical part of your value chain, relying on invoice price alone is a dangerous trap. Without objective data, purchasing decisions are driven by gut instinct, old habits, and the lowest bidder.

This subjective approach ignores the total cost of ownership. A "cheap" supplier who delivers late or sends poor quality goods is not saving you money, they are draining value from your chain.

To protect your margins, you must look beyond the price tag and assess the true cost of performance. **Without this visibility, you are effectively flying blind.**

![image/size:450px/align:right/frame:none](supplier-performance/Trap.png)
---
> [next] Continue -> 3

slide_id: 3
index_title: The cost of flying blind
title: **The cost of flying blind**
type: main
---
**And the cost of flying blind is high.**

When you manage suppliers without clear performance data, the consequences ripple through your entire organization.

Uncertainty forces you to hold expensive safety stock just to compensate for unreliability you haven't measured. Without shared data, you’re working in the dark - trading strategic planning for daily firefighting.

Ultimately, these inefficiencies surface as the worst possible outcome: empty shelves and disappointed customers.
![image/size:450px/align:right/frame:none](supplier-performance/Struggle.png)

[id:6/topic: Step 3: Drilling down to the products]

slide_id: 10
index_title: Step 3: Drilling down to the products
title: **Step 3: Drilling down to the products**
type: main
---
When you find a top supplier that is underperforming, you need to know exactly why. This is where the supplier framework and the inventory framework come together.

We drill down to the item level. Is the supplier's low turnover ratio caused by a massive amount of "dead" items sitting on your shelves? Or is their poor delivery score affecting your most critical A-items? Identifying the specific products gives you the exact leverage you need for your next vendor meeting.
