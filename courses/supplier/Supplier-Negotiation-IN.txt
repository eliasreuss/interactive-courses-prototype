[id:3/topic: Negotiating Based on ABC Categories]

slide_id: 6
index_title: AA and AB Products
title: **AA and AB Products**
layout: standard-2-col
type: main
---
For example, if your goal is earnings, you should focus on high-volume, high-value items - **AA and AB products.** Here, your key variables are cost price, volume and reliabiliy.

Because you buy large quantities, you have strong leverage. You can accept a higher MOQ (volume commitment) in exchange for the lowest possible cost price. Since demand is stable, the risk is low, and even a small reduction in cost price can have a significant impact. Having reliable deliveries is essential for customer satisfaction.

![image/size:650px/align:right/frame:none](learn/images/125/677e577f53e10411ea471b30478713e49813769ebd3bb09bc8c135723f5c2636)
---
> [next] Continue -> 7

slide_id: 7
index_title: CC, BC and AC Products
title: **CC, BC and AC Products**
layout: standard-2-col
type: main
---
On the other hand, if your goal is to reduce the amount of money tied up in inventory, you should focus on **BC and CC items.**

These are low-value items purchased infrequently. The goal is to stay flexible and avoid excess stock. Aim for short lead times, low minimum order quantities, or BTO/MTO setups. If a supplier cannot offer this, it is often easy to switch to one who can.

**AC items** are high-value and also purchased infrequently, which means they can tie up a lot of cash if they sit in stock. Focus on longer payment terms so you pay closer to when the item is sold, and try to reduce risk with lower MOQs or shorter lead times.
![image/size:650px/align:right/frame:none](learn/images/127/7c69958751a37f73a0c5be3cc99d27f3bc0fd7bdf83a7822ff57024a755be6c7)

slide_id: 8
index_title: CA and CB Products
title: **CA and CB Products**
layout: standard-2-col
type: main
---
**CA and CB items** are low-value products with steady demand. The goal is simple: keep them flowing without disruption.

Even though they do not drive large profits, running out can cause real problems, such as unsatisfied customers. Focus on reliability and efficiency to ensure stable supply, and aim for larger, less frequent orders to reduce administrative work.
![image/size:650px/align:right/frame:none](learn/images/126/46410b89ecfbb9d038a235add92f25344247f2a5613645de1ebbcf74104e7f95)
